🦕 How Dino Race Works

Your dinosaur's speed is driven entirely by your real-time internet connection quality — three metrics are measured from your browser and combined into a single velocity every 500ms.

RTT — Ping (ms)

Round-trip time: how quickly a small request travels from your browser to a server and back. Lower RTT = faster dino.

Measured by: fetch() to speed.cloudflare.com/cdn-cgi/trace with cache: 'no-store', sampled every 1.5 seconds. Uses Cloudflare's nearest Point of Presence (PoP) — this reflects real internet latency, not the game server's own tunnel overhead. A rolling window of the last 10 samples is averaged.

Half-speed threshold: 40 ms.

Jitter — Stability (ms)

How much your ping varies over time. A rock-steady connection scores well; a fluctuating one is penalised even if the average RTT looks decent.

Measured by: standard deviation of the same RTT rolling window of 10 samples.

Half-speed threshold: 20 ms of jitter. (Good Wi-Fi sits around 3–5 ms; heavy congestion or mobile can exceed 40 ms.)

Download Speed (Mbps)

How fast your connection can pull data. Gives a logarithmic boost — raw bandwidth matters, but with sharply diminishing returns once you're past ~50 Mbps.

Measured by: downloading 1 MB from speed.cloudflare.com/__down and timing it, every 8 seconds. Boost: +15% at 10 Mbps, +29% at 100 Mbps, +43% at 1 Gbps.

velocity = 45 × (1 / (1 + RTT / 40)) × (1 / (1 + jitter / 20)) × (1 + log₁₀(Mbps + 1) / 7) Race finishes at: 1,000 units Good broadband (~35 ms RTT, 10 ms jitter, 30 Mbps) → finishes comfortably Fibre (~10 ms RTT, 3 ms jitter, 300 Mbps) → wins convincingly Flaky mobile (~150 ms RTT, 40 ms jitter, 5 Mbps) → likely doesn't finish
